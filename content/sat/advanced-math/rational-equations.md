# Rational Equations — SAT Math Guide

Rational equations SAT problems appear frequently in the Advanced Math domain of the Digital SAT. These equations contain fractions with variables in the denominator, requiring you to solve for unknown values while avoiding solutions that make denominators zero. You'll encounter 2-3 rational equation questions on test day, making them a crucial skill for boosting your SAT math score. With the right approach, these problems become much more manageable than they first appear.

## What You Need to Know

• A rational equation contains one or more fractions with variables in the denominator
• Always identify restricted values (values that make denominators zero) before solving
• Cross-multiplication works when you have one fraction on each side of the equation
• For complex rational equations, multiply both sides by the LCD (least common denominator)
• Check all solutions in the original equation — some may be extraneous
• Extraneous solutions are values that solve the simplified equation but make denominators zero in the original

> 📐 KEY FORMULA: If a/b = c/d, then ad = bc (cross-multiplication)
> 💡 PRO TIP: Always check your answer by substituting back into the original equation — the College Board loves including extraneous solutions as trap answers.

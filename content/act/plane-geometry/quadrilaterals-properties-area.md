# Quadrilaterals Properties and Area — ACT Math Guide

Quadrilaterals properties and area ACT questions appear regularly on the math section, testing your knowledge of squares, rectangles, parallelograms, trapezoids, and rhombuses. These four-sided polygons show up in about 4-6 questions out of the 60 questions you'll face in 60 minutes. The good news is that once you know the key properties and area formulas, these problems become very manageable and can boost your ACT math score.

## What You Need to Know

• **Rectangle**: Opposite sides equal and parallel, all angles 90°, diagonals equal
• **Square**: All sides equal, all angles 90°, diagonals equal and perpendicular  
• **Parallelogram**: Opposite sides equal and parallel, opposite angles equal, diagonals bisect each other
• **Rhombus**: All sides equal, opposite angles equal, diagonals perpendicular and bisect each other
• **Trapezoid**: One pair of parallel sides (bases), other sides are legs
• **Area formulas**: Rectangle/square (l×w), parallelogram/rhombus (b×h), trapezoid (½×(b₁+b₂)×h)

> 📐 KEY FORMULA: Area of trapezoid = ½ × (base₁ + base₂) × height
> ⏱️ ACT TIME TIP: Draw and label quadrilaterals quickly — visual organization saves precious seconds when you have just 1 minute per question

## How to Solve Quadrilaterals Properties and Area on the ACT

### Example Question 1 — Easy/Medium Difficulty
A rectangle has a length of 12 inches and a width of 5 inches. What is the area of the rectangle in square inches?

A) 17
B) 34  
C) 60
D) 144
E) 169

**Solution:**
Step 1: Identify the shape and required formula (rectangle, so Area = length × width)
Step 2: Substitute the given values (Area = 12 × 5)
Step 3: Calculate the result (Area = 60 square inches)

✅ Answer: C — The area of a rectangle equals length times width, so 12 × 5 = 60 square inches.

### Example Question 2 — Hard Difficulty
A trapezoid has parallel bases of lengths 8 cm and 14 cm. If the area of the trapezoid is 66 square cm, what is the height?

A) 3 cm
B) 6 cm
C) 9 cm
D) 11 cm
E) 22 cm

**Solution:**
Step 1: Write the trapezoid area formula (Area = ½ × (b₁ + b₂) × h)
Step 2: Substitute known values (66 = ½ × (8 + 14) × h)
Step 3: Simplify and solve (66 = ½ × 22 × h, so 66 = 11h, therefore h = 6)

✅ Answer: B — Using the trapezoid area formula and solving for height gives us 6 cm.

## Common ACT Math Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Mistake: Confusing perimeter and area formulas under time pressure
✅ Fix: Remember area always involves multiplication, perimeter involves addition

❌ Mistake: Using side length instead of height for parallelogram area
✅ Fix: Height is always perpendicular to the base, not the slanted side length

❌ Mistake: Forgetting the ½ factor in trapezoid area calculations
✅ Fix: Write out the full formula first: Area = ½ × (b₁ + b₂) × h

❌ Mistake: Mixing up which sides are parallel in trapezoids
✅ Fix: The parallel sides are always the bases, regardless of orientation

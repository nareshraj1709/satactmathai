# Whole Numbers and Place Value — ACT Math Guide

Whole numbers and place value ACT questions form the foundation of your math skills on test day. These questions test your ability to understand how digits work in different positions and perform basic operations with large numbers. While place value appears in roughly 2-3 questions out of 60 on the ACT math section, mastering this concept builds confidence for more complex problems you'll face in your 60 minutes. You've got this — let's make place value second nature!

## What You Need to Know

• Place value determines a digit's worth based on its position in a number
• Each position represents a power of 10 (ones, tens, hundreds, thousands, etc.)
• Whole numbers include 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5... and continue infinitely
• Reading numbers correctly requires understanding comma placement every three digits
• Rounding follows the "5 or more, round up" rule
• Standard form uses digits, while word form spells out numbers completely

> 📐 KEY FORMULA: Place Value = Digit × Position Value (10⁰, 10¹, 10², 10³...)
> ⏱️ ACT TIME TIP: Don't second-guess yourself on basic place value — trust your instincts and move quickly to save time for harder questions later in the section.

## How to Solve Whole Numbers and Place Value on the ACT

### Example Question 1 — Easy/Medium Difficulty
What is the value of the digit 7 in the number 4,275,831?

A) 7
B) 70
C) 700
D) 7,000
E) 70,000

**Solution:**
Step 1: Identify the position of the digit 7 in 4,275,831
Step 2: Count from right to left: 1s, 10s, 100s, 1,000s, 10,000s
Step 3: The 7 is in the ten-thousands place, so its value is 7 × 10,000 = 70,000

✅ Answer: E — The digit 7 occupies the ten-thousands place, making its value 70,000.

### Example Question 2 — Hard Difficulty
A certain whole number, when rounded to the nearest thousand, equals 847,000. What is the smallest possible value of this whole number?

A) 846,500
B) 846,999
C) 847,000
D) 847,499
E) 847,500

**Solution:**
Step 1: Understand that rounding to 847,000 means the original number was between two boundaries
Step 2: Find the lower boundary: any number 846,500 or greater rounds up to 847,000
Step 3: The smallest whole number that rounds to 847,000 is exactly 846,500

✅ Answer: A — Numbers from 846,500 to 847,499 all round to 847,000, so 846,500 is the smallest.

## Common ACT Math Mistakes to Avoid

❌ Mistake: Confusing digit value with place value (saying 7 = 7 instead of 7 = 70,000)
✅ Fix: Always identify the position first, then multiply the digit by its place value

❌ Mistake: Miscounting positions when numbers have many digits
✅ Fix: Use commas as guides and count systematically from right to left

❌ Mistake: Rounding incorrectly by forgetting the "5 or more" rule
✅ Fix: Remember that 5 always rounds up, and anything less than 5 rounds down

❌ Mistake: Rushing through "easy" questions and making careless errors
✅ Fix: Even basic ACT math questions deserve careful attention — accuracy beats speed
